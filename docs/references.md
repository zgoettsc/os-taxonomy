# References — the learning-science foundation

The pedagogy behind this project draws on a specific lineage of cognitive-science
and talent-development work. We cite and link these rather than vendoring
copyrighted texts into the repo.

## Primary influences

- **Skycak, J. (with Math Academy).** *The Math Academy Way: Using the Power of
  Science to Supercharge Student Learning* (working draft, 2023–).
  <https://www.justinmath.com/books/> ·
  PDF: <https://www.justinmath.com/files/the-math-academy-way.pdf>
  The book-length system design: knowledge graph of "knowledge points",
  scaffolding, mastery learning, automaticity, and the spaced-repetition model
  below. Our synthesis: [`math-academy-notes.md`](math-academy-notes.md).

- **Skycak, J.** "Optimized, Individualized Spaced Repetition in Hierarchical
  Knowledge Structures."
  <https://www.justinmath.com/individualized-spaced-repetition-in-hierarchical-knowledge-structures/>
  Describes **Fractional Implicit Repetition (FIRe)** — spaced repetition that
  propagates "trickle-down" credit through prerequisite edges and minimizes
  reviews by having advanced reviews knock out due prerequisite reviews.

- **Skycak, J.** "Cognitive Science of Learning" series (spaced repetition,
  deliberate practice, cognitive load, etc.). <https://www.justinmath.com/>
  The shared essay reproduced in [`learning-science.md`](learning-science.md)
  is from this body of work.

## Foundational science these build on

- **Ebbinghaus, H. (1885).** *Memory: A Contribution to Experimental
  Psychology.* — the forgetting curve.
- **Ericsson, K. A., et al. (1993).** "The Role of Deliberate Practice in the
  Acquisition of Expert Performance." — deliberate practice.
- **Sweller, J. (1988– ).** Cognitive Load Theory; the worked-example effect and
  the **expertise reversal effect** (Kalyuga et al.).
- **Roediger, H. L. & Karpicke, J. D. (2006).** "Test-Enhanced Learning." — the
  testing / retrieval-practice effect.
- **Bjork, R. A.** "Desirable difficulties" and the distinction between learning
  and performance.
- **Bloom, B. S. (1984).** "The 2 Sigma Problem" — mastery learning + tutoring.

## How we use these

We are not affiliated with Math Academy. We treat their published methodology as
prior art and design inspiration, cite it, and re-implement compatible ideas
(prerequisite gating, spaced retrieval, implicit-repetition accounting) on top of
the openly-licensed Marble taxonomy. Terminology attributed to them (FIRe,
encompassing) is theirs; verify exact definitions against the source above.

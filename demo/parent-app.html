Fluent adding & subtracting within 5Math · NewNew

Fluently add and subtract within 5 — recall the answer quickly, without counting up on fingers each time.

What mastery looks likeAnswers 8 of 10 facts correctly in about 3 seconds each, on two different days.
Examples3 + 2 = 5 · 5 − 1 = 4 · 4 + 1 = 5 · 2 + 2 = 4
Number bonds to 5Math · ReviewReview

Know the pairs of numbers that make 5, and the matching subtraction facts, from memory.

What mastery looks likeGiven one part, names the other part of 5 without counting.
Examples1 + 4 · 2 + 3 · 0 + 5 · 5 − 2 = 3
Basic Nouns & VerbsEnglish · NewNew

Use frequently-occurring nouns (people, places, things) and verbs (action words) correctly in speech and writing.

What mastery looks likeSorts words into nouns vs verbs and uses each correctly in a spoken sentence.
ExamplesNouns: dog, park, cup · Verbs: run, jump, eat
Rhyming wordsEnglish · ReviewReview

Recognise and produce rhyming words; join in with predictable rhymes in stories and poems.

What mastery looks likeGives a word that rhymes with a spoken word 4 of 5 times.
Examplescat → hat · sun → run · tree → bee
Animal CamouflageScience · Cats unitUnit

Know that many animals use camouflage — colours and patterns that help them blend into their surroundings — to hide from predators or sneak up on prey.

What mastery looks likeExplains, in their own words, how camouflage helps one animal survive.
ExamplesStick insect looks like a twig · Polar bear is white in snow · A tabby cat hides in dry grass
